The greater Los Angeles metropolitan area is a city of cities, home to some 13 million people.

As with any world-city, diversity and geographical proximity makes it a complex system, a place where differences are constantly being negotiated. Fault Line is a directory of liminal spaces within the city; places that mark considerable statistical difference between adjacent urban areas.

Lines are drawn when there is a distinct difference in the demographics of 2 neighboring block groups.

We envision these Fault Lines as dynamic elements in the city, windfall zones that are the first to be effected by policy change and demographic shifts. They are also a meeting place between different lifestyles and therefore can suggest a a potential space for meaningful urban interventions.

We built Fault Line by juxtaposing different urban datasets visually. Through these multi-layered visualizations, we hope to aid our own understanding of the city's internal borders, and bring this new view to a popular audience

The data source for these visualizations are based on the American Community Survey 5 Year.

Street imagery is from Google Street View.

Neighborhood boundaries are from the Los Angeles Times- Data Desk
